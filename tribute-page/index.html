M.S Dhoni

Great Indian Cricketer and Former Captain of the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2017 and in Test cricket from 2008 to 2014. Considered one of the greatest cricket captains, wicket-keeper-batsman and finishers in the history of cricket. Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About the Legend

• MS Dhoni, in full Mahendra Singh Dhoni, was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India.

• He began playing cricket at a young age and initially excelled as a goalkeeper in football.

• He made his debut for the Indian cricket team in December 2004 in an ODI against Bangladesh.

• Known for his exceptional captaincy skills, cool demeanor under pressure, and strategic thinking.

• Led India to its first T20 World Cup victory in 2007.

• Captained the Indian team to win the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011 after a 28-year drought.

• He guided India to the ICC Champions Trophy victory in 2013 and due to this he was noted for his powerful and innovative batting, particularly his proficiency in finishing matches.

• Renowned for his lightning-fast reflexes and ability to effect quick stumpings.

• Holds the record for most dismissals by an Indian wicketkeeper in ODIs and T20Is.

• Set a new world record for the most dismissals by a wicketkeeper in a single innings in Test cricket.

• Considered one of India's most successful cricket captains, achieving numerous milestones during his tenure.

• Under his leadership, India achieved the No. 1 ranking in Test cricket for the first time.

• His calm demeanor and tactical acumen earned him the nickname "Captain Cool."

• Popularized the "helicopter shot," a unique and powerful batting technique, which became his signature move.

• Regarded as one of the greatest cricketing legends of his generation. His leadership and contributions have inspired a new generation of cricketers in India.

• Remains an icon and a role model for aspiring cricketers and fans alike.

• Dhoni's philanthropic efforts include support for various charitable causes and initiatives.

• An honorary lieutenant colonel in the Indian Territorial Army, reflecting his commitment beyond cricket.
